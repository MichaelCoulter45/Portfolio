About Me

Developer, builder

Who I am

I'm Michael Coulter. I'm a software developer with a passion for building things that are actually useful. I gravitate toward projects that involve automation, data, and solving problems that feel tedious to do by hand.

Outside of coding, I'm a serious marathon runner. I've been training year-round for three years now, and that discipline bleeds into everything I do including how I approach building software. It's a long game, and so is writing good code.

Background & goals

I got into development by wanting to automate things that bothered me, like repetitive tasks in games, messy data I wanted to visualize, workflows I wanted to streamline. Every project I've built has started from a genuine personal need.

My goal is to keep growing as a developer, deepen my skills in data engineering and analytics, and eventually work on projects that sit at the intersection of performance data and real-world decision making whether that's in sports, fitness, or beyond.

The running connection

Marathon training generates a huge amount of data. Each run has pace, heart rate, cadence, elevation, and power - just to name a few. Most of the tools out there don't give you full control over that data. That's why I started building the Runner's Dashboard: a personal analytics platform for my own training, built from scratch.

It's the project I care most about, and it's the one that pushes me hardest as a developer.
